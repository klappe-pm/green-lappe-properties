# 2026-05-18-1230

## Name

**Green Lappe Properties.** Brand system, tokens, and style guide drafted. Two-sided marketplace framing established (owners + residents/operators as both customers, company owns the operating layer).

## What's Decided

- **Geography**: King and Snohomish counties, first year confined to a single corridor (Bothell, Mill Creek, Kirkland, Kenmore, Lynnwood).
- **Scope**: SFR, condo, townhome, small multifamily (<20 units). No HOA, no commercial, no large multifamily.
- **Wedge**: standard residential management as volume product; licensed family child care (FCC) conversions as margin product.
- **Pricing posture**: transparent flat or low percentage management fee, capped placement fee, lower renewal fee, application fee at WA statutory cap with no markup, no undisclosed vendor kickbacks.
- **Differentiator**: operational discipline (named partner lines, monthly owner packet, published rules), not custom software.
- **Tech posture**: buy not build. Rentvine, Buildium, or AppFolio for PMS. TransUnion SmartMove for screening.
- **North-star metric**: leased months (defined in `leased-months-north-star.md`), with a counter-metrics set already specified.
- **Founding asset**: one Bothell property in portfolio, designated as the founding listing.

## What's Researched and Sitting in Project

- TAM: ~$1.55B at 10% fee benchmark across the two counties (2025).
- Competitor landscape: 40+ independent firms identified, PE/institutional firms enumerated for exclusion.
- Customer pain points: documented across three personas (accidental landlord, small-portfolio investor, out-of-state owner) with named-source quotes.
- Rate-locked landlord opportunity: structural thesis written, pricing tiers proposed (9–10% for legacy holders, 7–8% with leasing-fee upside for recent high-rate investors).
- Brand system: tokens, style guide, voice, fee transparency promise.
- Operations plan: licensing chain, banking (trust account requirement), insurance stack (E&O, GL, cyber, EPLI, crime), vendor qualification checklist, 180-day Gantt.
- CAC model and metrics glossary: drafted.

## What Isn't Decided (open from `green-lappe-two-sided-marketplace.md` §12)

2. FCC wedge: sub-brand or kept under Green Lappe Properties.
3. Whether to accept owners switching from another PM at a discount during year one.
4. Whether to publish anonymized marketplace metrics as a trust signal.

6. Owners who want to keep their existing PMS account: accommodate or require migration.
7. Whether the company ever holds its own inventory or stays pure operating layer.

## Gating Constraint

Designated broker endorsement. WA DOL requires a managing broker with three years active brokerage experience plus 90 clock hours managing broker education plus the managing broker exam, before the firm license attaches. If the founder does not personally hold this, the firm cannot license; an outside DB hire adds $80–150K plus benefits to year-one cash burn. This is the binary go/no-go.

## Startup Capital Floor (from Ops plan)

One-time ~$50–95K. Year-one ongoing ~$36–65K. Excludes founder salary, outside DB if applicable, and any portfolio-acquisition signing bonuses. 6-month working capital reserve is in the one-time number.
