# effort-4-narrative

## Executive summary

King and Snohomish renter demand is anchored by five sectors. **Tech (Microsoft, Amazon, Google, Meta and the next tier) drives roughly 38-44% of net renter demand** in the two counties, concentrated in Bellevue, Redmond, Kirkland, SLU, and Downtown Seattle. **Aerospace (Boeing + Crane + Heico) drives ~12-16% of renter demand**, concentrated in Everett, Mukilteo, Renton, Tukwila, Auburn. **Healthcare (Providence, Swedish, UW Medicine, Fred Hutch, Seattle Children's, MultiCare) drives ~14-18%**, broadly distributed. **Retail HQ (Costco, Starbucks, Nordstrom) drives ~6-9%**, concentrated in Issaquah + Seattle SODO + Downtown. **Public sector (UW, City/County government, Port of Seattle) drives ~10-14%**, broadly distributed. The residual 8-12% is non-tech-non-aerospace private sector (PACCAR, Weyerhaeuser, Premera, Alaska Air, professional services).

The 2022-2023 tech layoff cycle and the 2024-2025 follow-on cycle show that **tech-cohort renter demand is highly elastic to layoffs in Class A urban Bellevue/SLU/Downtown but largely insulated in small-portfolio Class B/C anywhere in the two counties**. The Class B/C insulation thesis is the core operational finding. Snohomish and South King small-portfolio inventory has the strongest insulation because non-tech employer demand dominates the local tenant pool.

The Boeing 737 MAX production rate cap (38/month after the Jan-2024 Alaska 1282 door plug incident) and the Nov-2024 10% global cut (post-IAM 751 strike) represent the largest concentrated risk to Snohomish small-portfolio TAM. If Boeing follows through on further wide-body slowdowns through 2026, Snohomish renter demand could move by 4-6% net in 12-18 months.

## Boeing Snohomish exposure analysis

Boeing is the single largest concentrated risk to Snohomish renter demand. The exposure operates through three channels.

**Channel 1: Direct Boeing employment.** ~22,000 Boeing employees in Snohomish (Everett 98204 widebody assembly + Mukilteo 98275 fabrication + Everett 98203 office and engineering). Boeing renter share is ~28% (lower than tech because median tenure is longer and home-purchase rate is higher). Renter household estimate from direct Boeing: ~6,200 in Snohomish.

**Channel 2: Boeing supplier ecosystem.** Crane Aerospace, Heico subsidiaries, KCMA, Electroimpact, Vector Aerospace, Triumph Group, regional MRO providers, Spirit AeroSystems (now Boeing again post Jul-2024 reacquisition). Estimated 18,000-24,000 supplier employees in Snohomish primarily exposed to Boeing programs. Supplier renter share ~38% (younger workforce, more contract-based). Supplier renter household estimate: ~7,500 in Snohomish.

**Channel 3: Induced spending.** Boeing + supplier total payroll in Snohomish ~$3.5B/yr. The induced employment multiplier (BEA Regional Input-Output Model) is ~1.6x for Boeing payroll in WA, translating to ~30,000-40,000 induced jobs in Snohomish + adjacent King. The induced workers are retail, hospitality, healthcare, services — they have renter share ~52% and tend to live in Snohomish small-portfolio inventory. Induced renter household estimate: ~17,000 in Snohomish.

Total Snohomish renter household exposure to Boeing direct + supplier + induced: **~30,700**.

Snohomish total renter households ~210,000 (King+Sno total 770k; Snohomish share ~27%). Boeing-exposed share: **~14.6% of Snohomish renter households**.

### Boeing scenarios for 2026-2027

**Base case (50% probability).** 737 MAX rate cap holds at 38/mo through 2026; 777X first delivery slips to late-2026 or 2027 (already slipped 5+ years from original 2020 target); 787 stays at ~5/mo. Boeing WA headcount holds at ~66k. Snohomish renter demand stable.

**Downside case (30% probability).** FAA further restricts 737 MAX rate after another quality incident; 777X delivery slips further; Boeing executes a second 10% global cut. Snohomish Boeing direct + supplier cuts ~4,000-6,000. Induced effect: another ~6,000-8,000 jobs softer. Snohomish renter demand drops 4-7% in 12-18 months. Class A and Class B/C Everett rents move down ~3-5%; vacancy moves up ~150-250bps.

**Upside case (20% probability).** 737 MAX rate cap lifted in 2026 to 42/mo then 47/mo; 777X enters service Q4-2026 with strong delivery cadence; Boeing hires back 3,000-5,000 in WA. Snohomish renter demand strengthens; Everett small-portfolio rents grow 4-6%; vacancy compresses 100-150bps.

### Operational implication

Snohomish small-portfolio acquisition pricing should incorporate a **15-20% Boeing-volatility discount** relative to comparable King County Class B/C inventory. The discount is partially offset by lower acquisition prices and lower regulatory friction in unincorporated Snohomish + Everett + Mukilteo + Lynnwood.

**The Snohomish small-portfolio thesis is aerospace-anchored, not tech-anchored, and Green Property Management should plan its monitoring layer around Boeing program-rate disclosures + IAM 751 + SPEEA contract cycles.** The Boeing 10-K, the FAA monthly delivery reports, and the IAM/SPEEA contract status pages should be quarterly review items.

## Amazon Bellevue tower expansion exposure analysis

Amazon's Bellevue physical-presence trajectory is the single largest concentrated risk to Bellevue urban core renter demand. The exposure operates as follows.

**The expansion.** Amazon committed to 25,000+ employees in Bellevue by 2025 across Bellevue 600 (Tower), 555 Tower, West Main, Bellevue 555, Bellevue Tower 24, Centre 425, and adjacent buildings. As of late 2025, Amazon has ~25,000 employees in Bellevue (subject to revision after the Oct-2025 14k corporate cut concentrated on Puget Sound).

**Renter household impact.** Amazon Bellevue employees have an estimated 78% renter share (skewed by Cohort A + D dominance + young workforce). The Bellevue Amazon renter household estimate: **~19,500**. This is ~22% of all Bellevue (98004 + 98005 + 98006 + 98007 + 98008) renter households.

**The volatility.** The Oct-2023 RTO 3-day mandate, the Sep-2024 5-day RTO mandate (effective Jan-2025), and the Oct-2025 14k corporate cut interact destructively. The 5-day mandate compresses commute tolerance, pushing Amazon employees toward Bellevue Class A or close-in Class B/C — supportive of Bellevue renter demand. The Oct-2025 cut redistributes ~2,000-2,500 Bellevue Amazon workers into Cohort E.

**Net 2026 trajectory.** Bellevue Amazon headcount likely stabilizes at 22,000-24,000 by end-2026 after Oct-2025 cut absorption. Renter share holds at ~78%. **Bellevue Amazon renter household estimate 2026: 17,000-18,800, down from ~19,500 peak in 2025.** The decline is roughly a 4-6% drag on Bellevue urban core renter demand isolated to 2026.

### Operational implication

Bellevue 98004 and 98007 Class A inventory is **most exposed** to any subsequent Amazon WA cut. A second Amazon WA cut of similar scale (~2,800+ WA WARN) in 2026 or 2027 would drive Bellevue Class A vacancy materially. Bellevue Class B/C is partly insulated by the Class A → Class B/C downshift pattern documented in the 2022-2023 cycle, but only partly: the downshift's tailwind for Class B/C is smaller than the headwind for Class A.

**Green Property Management should treat Bellevue 98004 + 98007 small-portfolio Class B/C as moderate risk during 2026-2027**, with the operational plan being to retain Cohort A + D tenants through lease renewal incentives + accept slightly higher screening accommodations for laid-off Amazon employees on severance.

## Boeing 2024 IAM 751 strike and production outlook

The 2024 IAM 751 strike began Sep-13-2024 and ended Nov-4-2024 (53 days). The settlement: 38% wage increase over 4 years (cumulative; ~13% Year 1, then ~6% per year), $12k ratification bonus, modest pension contributions, no return of defined-benefit pension (frozen 2014). Affected ~33,000 IAM members in WA primarily at Renton 737 plant + Everett 777/767/777X widebody + Auburn fabrication.

The post-strike production outlook through 2026:

- **737 MAX:** FAA-imposed 38/mo rate cap holds through 2026 because of quality-system audit requirements after Jan-2024 Alaska 1282 incident. Renton plant is running below cap (~32-35/mo actual) through Q1-2026 with ramp toward cap by Q3-2026.
- **777X:** First delivery slipped from 2025 to late-2026 / early-2027. Everett widebody hours are partially preserved by ongoing 787 + 767 work but the 777X delay drags Snohomish headcount.
- **787:** Charleston SC remains primary final assembly; Everett does some sub-assembly. Rate ~5/mo holds.

The Nov-2024 10% global cut (Boeing announcement Oct-2024) was the post-strike rationalization. Affected ~3,200 in WA: ~2,200 Snohomish (Everett + Mukilteo widebody-adjacent) + ~1,000 King (Renton 737 + Auburn fabrication + Tukwila). The cuts hit middle-management + indirect labor primarily; IAM and SPEEA represented workers were largely protected.

## Sub-markets to monitor for next-cycle dynamics

The Class A vs Class B/C insulation thesis is the core finding. The four next-cycle dynamics Green Property Management should monitor:

1. **5-day RTO enforcement.** Microsoft (effective Feb-2025), Amazon (effective Jan-2025), Starbucks (effective Jan-2025), T-Mobile (effective hybrid-3 holding), and how strict actual enforcement becomes by mid-2026. Strong enforcement reverses the 2021-2023 sub-urban migration; weak enforcement preserves it.
2. **AI hiring composition shift.** Microsoft, Amazon, Google, Anthropic, OpenAI are shifting hiring composition toward applied scientists + ML engineers (higher comp, smaller cohort) and away from junior SDEs (Cohort A inflow shrinks). This compresses Cohort A density in Bellevue/SLU.
3. **Boeing program-rate variance.** If 737 MAX rate is lifted above 38/mo through 2026, Snohomish small-portfolio rent growth lifts 100-200bps. If a second incident triggers further restriction, Snohomish renter demand drops 4-7%.
4. **H-1B policy variance.** Federal immigration policy outcomes affect Cohort D inflow directly. A meaningful reduction in H-1B caps (~20%+ cut) would compress Cohort D by ~10k-15k in 24 months, primarily impacting Bellevue 98004+98007+98008 + Redmond 98052 Class A.

## Caveats and methodology limits

- Employer headcount figures are triangulated from PSBJ Largest Employers, 10-K filings, and LinkedIn workforce signals; LinkedIn alone is unreliable so cross-check is required.
- Tech compensation is triangulated across levels.fyi, Blind, USCIS LCA prevailing wage, and BLS OEWS; reported ranges, not point estimates.
- Cohort counts are modeled, not measured. The cohort definitions overlap (Cohort A and D share ~40% membership; Cohort C and F share residual entries).
- WARN filings are minimum-count signals; actual layoff counts are typically higher because WARN exempts smaller cuts <50 at a single worksite and contractors.
- LCA worksite ZIPs are postal ZIPs; mapping to Effort 0 ZCTAs uses the HUD USPS postal-to-ZCTA crosswalk. For the four cross-county Pierce ZCTAs (98001, 98022, 98047, 98092) the pct_land_king weighting noted in Effort 0 has been applied for any allocation needs but no LCA worksite of consequence falls in the small Pierce-county slivers.
- Headcount allocations across cross-county boundaries assume pct_land_king-weighted distribution at the ZCTA level. No employer of size in this data set has a Pierce-side worksite at 98001/98022/98047/98092.
- The 2022-2023 layoff-cycle DiD result of -8.7pp Bellevue Class A vs Renton Class A face-rent attributable component drops to -3.5 to -5.0pp after netting supply, rate, and re-urbanization confounders; the Class B/C insulation thesis is more robust to these confounders.
- Headcount for Boeing in WA cited here (~66,000) is approximate; Boeing's exact WA employment is reported in WA OFM employer reports and varies quarter-to-quarter.

## Conclusion

The five operational outputs of Effort 4 for Green Property Management:

1. **Diversify away from Bellevue 98004 + 98007 + SLU 98109 + Downtown 98101 + Redmond 98052** concentration in the first 100-500 doors. These sub-markets are tech-cycle-exposed and recently lower-yielding due to Class A supply.
2. **Concentrate in Federal Way, Auburn, Renton, Edmonds, Everett, Lynnwood, Mountlake Terrace, Kent small-portfolio Class B/C inventory** for the most cycle-insulated TAM.
3. **Treat Mukilteo and Marysville as aerospace-exposed**; acquire only with explicit Boeing-cycle hedge (lower acquisition price ~15-20% relative to comparable insulated inventory + monitor Boeing 737 MAX rate quarterly).
4. **Monitor monthly the Apartment List concession index for Bellevue 98004, SLU 98109, Downtown 98101, Redmond 98052** as a 60-90 day leading indicator for Class B/C pricing power in adjacent sub-markets.
5. **Build employer-direct relationships with Microsoft, Amazon, Boeing, Providence, MultiCare relocation teams** for inbound new-hire referral pipelines — this captures Cohort A + D + healthcare inflow before the listing channel.
