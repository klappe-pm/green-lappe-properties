# Green Lappe Properties: Partner Opportunity Brief

Audience: Megan Green. Built from the local project corpus only; no web search used.

## The Partner Thesis

Green Lappe Properties should not launch as a generic property management company or a venture-style roll-up. The stronger thesis is narrower: a two-principal, owner-operated firm that starts with Snohomish and north King single-family homes, uses Megan's local trust graph for distribution, and uses Kevin's systems background to make a small portfolio feel more accountable than larger incumbents.

The first product is not scale. The first product is proof that owners and residents can both experience named accountability, transparent fees, and documented response standards.

## What the Corpus Says

| Lens | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Market scale | The combined King + Snohomish small-owner rental pool is modeled at about 302,000 units and $7.636B gross rent roll. At a 10% management-fee tier, full-stock TAM is $763.6M annually. |
| Greenfield | Roughly 78% of small-owner units are modeled as self-managed, creating a $595.6M ongoing greenfield TAM at a 10% fee tier. |
| Practical PM market | The reconciled two-county base PM market is $219M annually: King $185M and Snohomish $34M. |
| Fragmentation | No single in-scope King operator exceeds 7% of SAM; the top five together hold about 11%. |
| Launch choice | Data ranks King/Eastside/Seattle highly for PM sequencing, but the GTM memo recommends Snohomish-first because Megan's network, SFR experience, and lower regulatory complexity make it the better proof ground. |
| First proof | Phase 1 target: 2-5 SFRs under management in months 1-12; 100% owner retention; at least 95% habitability SLA adherence; zero owner-facing fee disputes. |

Sources: `docs/research/unit-economics/Total Addressable Market (TAM).md`, `docs/research/competitors/Non-Institutional Small Landlord PM Market, King and Snohomish Counties.md`, `docs/gtm/Launch Prioritization.md`.

## Why Megan Matters

Megan is the launch advantage. The pitch prep memo identifies 21 years of local real estate experience, 108 career transactions, 17 transactions in the last 12 months, a 5.0 public review profile, a vendor/contractor network, and direct service-area overlap with Snohomish, north King, and Eastside target corridors.

That means the first cohort does not need paid acquisition. It needs careful conversion of existing trust: recent buyers, recent sellers, current owners relocating, and referral partners who already know Megan can get hard property work done.

## Why Kevin Matters

Kevin is the systems leverage layer. The corpus frames the core hypothesis as: two principals can manage 150-300 doors at higher owner NPS, longer renter tenure, and materially better contribution margin than incumbent PM firms if compliance, owner reporting, vendor workflow, and communication triage are designed from day one.

That remains an assumption. The next proof should not be a claim. It should be a working artifact: compliance calendar, owner report prototype, or maintenance SLA workflow.

## The Wedge

Start with transparent SFR management. Preserve the FCC wedge for margin expansion.

The FCC strategy scores the wedge as strong on Delight, Hard to Copy, and Margin. Structure B, a lease plus services agreement, is the preferred default after core PM quality is proven. That sequencing matters: FCC is differentiated and high-margin, but it should not absorb launch bandwidth before the basic PM promise is working.

## The First 90 Days

| Window | Work | Decision it unlocks |
|---|---|---|
| Days 1-30 | Confirm licensing/designated broker path, entity path, and outside employment constraints. | Whether the business can legally launch and who signs. |
| Days 1-45 | Run 20 owner discovery conversations through Megan's warm network. | Whether the first 2-5 doors are real. |
| Days 30-60 | Choose pricing posture: flat fee or premium percent-of-rent with no maintenance markup. | Whether the brand promise and economics match. |
| Days 45-75 | Build one systems proof point. | Whether the two-principal leverage claim is credible. |
| Days 60-90 | Select first properties, vendor bench, and SLA scorecard. | Whether to launch the first cohort. |

## Open Questions

1. Does Megan already meet the designated broker path, or is a third-party DB required?
2. What fee model is most credible for the first 2-5 owners?
3. What capacity ceiling can two principals defend after modeling per-door hours?
4. How will Megan's brokerage activity and PM activity be separated legally and operationally?
5. What proof artifact should Kevin build first?
6. Which first property becomes the case study?

## Recommendation

Proceed with a 90-day proof sprint, not a full company launch. The goal is to turn the corpus into operating evidence: first conversations, first pricing decision, first systems proof, first 2-5 properties, and a shared go/no-go decision for the partnership.
