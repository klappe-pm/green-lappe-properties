# Partner Pitch Deck Source

Deck title: `Green Lappe Properties: Partner Opportunity`

Audience: Megan Green.

Method: Nancy Duarte DataStory-style flow: shared situation, tension, data-backed insight, new possibility, action path.

## Slide 1: The Decision

**Claim:** Green Lappe is ready for a 90-day partner proof sprint.

**Proof object:** Metric rail: $763.6M full-stock TAM, $219M practical two-county PM market, 2-5 homes year-one proof, 90-day sprint.

**Notes:** Open with the decision, not the origin story. Megan already knows the pain. This deck asks whether the two of you should commit to proof.

**Sources:** TAM memo, non-institutional PM market memo, launch prioritization.

## Slide 2: What Is vs What Could Be

**Claim:** The market pain is clear; the opportunity is to operationalize the opposite.

**Proof object:** Two-column DataStory contrast.

**What is:** communication black holes, fee opacity, regulatory overload, overloaded PM staff.

**What could be:** named accountability, transparent owner packet, SLA-visible maintenance, compliance as product.

**Sources:** pain points, user journey map.

## Slide 3: The Market Is Big Enough

**Claim:** Market size is not the constraint.

**Proof object:** Horizontal bars: $763.6M TAM, $595.6M greenfield TAM, $219M practical PM market, $34M Snohomish PM base.

**Notes:** Reconcile broad TAM and practical PM market. Do not over-sell the largest number.

**Sources:** TAM memo, non-institutional PM market memo.

## Slide 4: Fragmentation Creates Entry Room

**Claim:** Green Lappe does not need to beat a dominant incumbent.

**Proof object:** Concentration table: top five at 11% of SAM, no operator above 7%.

**Notes:** The market can support a focused entrant. Entry quality matters more than share capture.

**Sources:** non-institutional PM market memo.

## Slide 5: Start Where Trust Converts Fastest

**Claim:** Launch should be Snohomish-first, SFR-first, Megan-network-first.

**Proof object:** Execution decision matrix comparing Snohomish launch to King/Seattle rank density.

**Notes:** Explain why the ZCTA scoring does not dictate launch order. Use data for expansion; use trust for launch.

**Sources:** launch prioritization, effort-6 final report.

## Slide 6: The Partner Fit Is the Asset

**Claim:** Megan brings the local trust graph; Kevin brings the leverage layer.

**Proof object:** Role split: trust/distribution/property judgment vs systems/compliance/reporting/workflow.

**Notes:** Keep this collaborative. This is not about credit. It is about division of labor and where each person reduces launch risk.

**Sources:** pitch deck prep, Kevin resume.

## Slide 7: First 50 Doors Is a Funnel, Not a Marketing Plan

**Claim:** The first growth path should come from warm owner relationships.

**Proof object:** Funnel: 108 career relationships, 20 discovery calls, 5-8 high-intent prospects, 2-5 launch homes, 6-12 phase-2 homes.

**Notes:** The numbers below 108 are assumptions to validate, not facts. Mark them as proof-sprint targets.

**Sources:** pitch deck prep, launch prioritization.

## Slide 8: The Operating System

**Claim:** The service promise needs systems before scale.

**Proof object:** Operating map: compliance calendar, owner packet, maintenance SLA, vendor workflow, inspection record.

**Notes:** This slide makes Kevin's role concrete. It also prevents "AI" from sounding vague.

**Sources:** pitch deck prep, user journey map, GTM plan.

## Slide 9: Boutique Economics Can Be Worth Building

**Claim:** A capped two-principal firm can still be economically meaningful.

**Proof object:** Door/revenue chart: 50, 150, 200, 250 doors at roughly $3,900 revenue/door/year.

**Notes:** Keep capacity as an assumption. The point is not that 250 is guaranteed; the point is that the economics justify the proof sprint.

**Sources:** pitch deck prep, PM market memo.

## Slide 10: FCC Is the Margin Wedge, Not the Launch Product

**Claim:** Preserve FCC optionality after the core PM promise works.

**Proof object:** Sequencing bar: core PM proof, phase-2 portfolio, first FCC candidate, repeatable FCC playbook.

**Notes:** FCC is the differentiated future. It should not distract from first-cohort operating proof.

**Sources:** FCC strategy, launch prioritization.

## Slide 11: What We Still Need to Prove

**Claim:** The plan is credible only if the assumptions are tested directly.

**Proof object:** Risk/assumption table: licensing, pricing, capacity, demand, role conflicts, first case study.

**Notes:** This is the anti-hype slide. It should increase trust.

**Sources:** pitch deck prep, GTM plan, market deep dive.

## Slide 12: 90-Day Proof Sprint

**Claim:** The next move is a proof sprint with explicit go/no-go criteria.

**Proof object:** 30/60/90 timeline and decision checklist.

**Notes:** End with a clear action path. The ask is partnership focus and decision ownership, not capital first.

**Sources:** two-pager, launch prioritization.
