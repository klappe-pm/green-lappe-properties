# DataStory Claim Spine

Method posture: use data to move from shared pain to a clear decision. The story alternates between "what is" and "what could be," then closes with a specific action path.

## Big Idea

Green Lappe can start as a deliberately small, owner-operated property management firm for Snohomish and north King single-family owners, then use compliance discipline, transparent economics, and the FCC wedge to become more valuable than a commodity PM book.

## Audience

Megan Green, future partner. She brings local trust, transaction flow, property judgment, and owner relationships. The package should help her evaluate whether to commit time, reputation, and partnership energy.

## What Is

Small-owner property management in King and Snohomish counties is fragmented, mistrusted, and operationally brittle. Owners see opaque fees and compliance landmines. Residents see maintenance silence and deposit fights. PM staff are overloaded. The market is large, but the standard model is not designed around trust or continuity.

## What Could Be

A two-principal firm can start with a narrow first cohort, name the accountable human, publish transparent economics, and use systems to reduce the work that normally forces PM firms into underpaid staff and opaque processes. The launch does not need to win two counties. It needs to prove that the promise works on 2-5 homes, then 6-12, then a capacity-tested portfolio.

## Tension

| Current state | Better future |
|---|---|
| Owners manage the manager. | Owners get a same-business-day answer and a monthly owner packet that reconciles. |
| Residents submit tickets into silence. | Residents see SLA status, named vendors, and documented repairs. |
| PM economics reward churn and markups. | Green Lappe earns from retention, clean documentation, and transparent fees. |
| Seattle complexity overwhelms operators. | Snohomish proves the operating model first; King complexity becomes a premium capability later. |
| FCC is an idea, not a launch product. | FCC becomes the margin-expansion wedge after core PM quality is proven. |

## The Ask

Commit to a 90-day proof sprint:

1. Confirm licensing and designated broker path.
2. Run 20 owner discovery conversations through Megan's warm network.
3. Select the first pricing posture.
4. Build one visible systems proof point: compliance calendar, owner packet prototype, or maintenance SLA workflow.
5. Choose the first 2-5 candidate homes.

## STAR Moment

The useful memorable point is not "large TAM." It is: Green Lappe does not need to become a roll-up to be worth building. At 200 doors and roughly $3,900 blended annual revenue per door, the corpus model implies about $780K in annual revenue before the FCC wedge. If quality holds, that is a real two-principal business and a valuable PM book.
