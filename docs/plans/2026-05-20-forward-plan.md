# Green Lappe Properties Forward Plan

Generated: 2026-05-20 09:30 PDT

## Objective

Move Green Lappe Properties from research-backed opportunity to a legally viable, partner-aligned, operating proof. The next phase should prove whether Megan's trust graph plus Kevin's systems layer can support a differentiated small-owner SFR property management business before committing to broader scale or the FCC wedge.

## Planning principles

- Treat the first 90 days as a proof sprint, not a company-scale launch.
- Keep the launch wedge narrow: Snohomish and adjacent north King SFR owners in Megan's network.
- Keep FCC as a later moat unless a uniquely strong property/provider pair appears.
- Keep broad TAM/SAM/SOM separate from proof-sprint capacity.
- Prefer owner-visible proof artifacts over more deck work.
- Do not scale doors until legal setup, workload model, and service standards are proven.

Deliverables:

## Phase 1: Legal and launch gates

Target: days 1-30.

Deliverables:

- Designated broker path decision.
- Entity structure decision.
- Operating role split between Megan and Kevin.
- Outside-activity clearance for Kevin if needed.
- Insurance, trust accounting, and compliance checklist.
- Launch go/no-go memo for legal readiness.

Questions:

- Does Megan already have the license path needed for firm-level PM activity?
- Is a third-party designated broker needed, and if so, what economics and control tradeoffs come with that?
- What activities can Kevin perform before any licensing or employment constraints are cleared?
- What jurisdiction-specific compliance surface applies to the first target properties?

## Phase 2: Customer proof

Target: days 1-45.

Deliverables:

- 20 owner discovery conversations from Megan's warm network.
- Named first-cohort prospect list with owner need, property type, location, timing, and pricing reaction.
- Decision on whether first 2-5 properties are real enough to proceed.
- Objection log and quote bank for future positioning.

Questions:

- Which owners have an urgent reason to switch or hire PM now?
- What are they dissatisfied with: fees, communication, maintenance, compliance, vacancy, or something else?
- Will owners pay for transparency and named accountability?
- What minimum proof do they need before giving Green Lappe a property?

## Phase 3: Pricing and economics

Target: days 30-60.

Deliverables:

- Pricing model decision: flat-fee premium or premium percent-of-rent with no maintenance markup.
- First-cohort unit economics model.
- Owner packet with fee policy, approval thresholds, invoice transparency, and renewal/leasing terms.
- Red-line list of fees Green Lappe will not charge.

Questions:

- Does a flat fee protect trust and margin better than a percentage model for high-rent SFRs?
- What maintenance threshold requires owner approval?
- Will "no hidden maintenance markup" become a durable brand position or leave too much margin on the table?
- What does the first owner contract need to say to avoid fee disputes?

## Phase 4: Systems proof

Target: days 45-75.

Deliverables:

- One owner-visible systems artifact selected from:
  - owner monthly packet prototype;
  - Washington compliance calendar and notice workflow;
  - maintenance SLA dashboard and work order workflow;
  - inspection and condition-documentation packet.
- Workload model for 2-5, 6-12, 25-50, and 150-250 doors.
- Service standard scorecard.

Questions:

- Which artifact most directly proves Kevin's systems value to Megan and to owners?
- Which workflows must exist before the first property is onboarded?
- At what door count does the two-principal model break without staff or contractors?
- What data must be captured from day one to prove service quality?

## Phase 5: First cohort decision

Target: days 60-90.

Deliverables:

- First property shortlist.
- Vendor bench and escalation tree.
- Onboarding checklist.
- Owner communication cadence.
- Resident maintenance SLA.
- Go/no-go recommendation for accepting first 2-5 managed homes.

Questions:

- Which property is safe enough to become the first case study?
- What property types or owner situations should be rejected at launch?
- What would cause Green Lappe to pause after the first property?
- What is the first public proof point after launch?

## Gaps to close before scale

- Legal/licensing readiness.
- Owner demand evidence.
- Pricing acceptance.
- Workload model.
- Trust-accounting and compliance operations.
- Vendor reliability.
- Resident support model.
- Documented service standards.
- Clear split between PM core and FCC optionality.

## Measures of success

- 20 owner conversations completed and summarized.
- 2-5 credible first-cohort properties identified.
- Legal launch path confirmed in writing.
- Pricing model chosen and reflected in an owner packet.
- One systems proof artifact ready to show an owner.
- First-cohort workload model accepted by both partners.
- Go/no-go decision documented before property onboarding begins.

## Measures that should not be overclaimed

- Broad TAM/SAM/SOM should not be treated as near-term capacity.
- Modeled ZIP rankings should not replace parcel-level underwriting.
- FCC revenue upside should not be treated as day-one PM revenue.
- Deck polish should not be treated as operating readiness.
