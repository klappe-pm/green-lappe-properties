# Licensing And Compliance

- Date: 2026-07-20
- Status: DRAFT
- Parent: [[../childcare-operator-property-model-strategy|Childcare Operator Property Model Strategy]]
- Implements: Licensing and compliance

## Objective

Ensure every host property is licensable before lease and licensed before operation, with the operator holding the license, so no site ever operates outside the rules.

## Scope

In scope: the licensing pathway, the property conditions licensing requires, and the compliance checkpoints. Out of scope: financial underwriting (see property sourcing and underwriting).

## Steps

1. Encode the license classes as a capacity rule: small Family Child Care Home up to 8 children with the provider as sole caregiver, large up to 14 with a paid assistant required above 6 children (California; re-source for Washington under D1).
2. Turn the license requirements into a property-conditions checklist used in the sourcing licensing-feasibility screen.
3. Define the sequence: lease to a license-eligible operator, support the license application, and permit operation only after the license is granted.
4. Set a recurring compliance check that no operating site lacks a current license.

## Acceptance

- No property is leased for childcare use without a passing licensing-feasibility verdict.
- No site operates without a current license, verified on a recurring check.

## Measures

- Time from lease to license granted.
- Count of operating sites without a current license, target zero.

## Open questions

- D1 geography determines the licensing authority and the exact capacity and staffing rules.
