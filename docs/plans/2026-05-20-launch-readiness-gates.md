# Launch Readiness Gates

Purpose: turn the launch-readiness backlog into a concrete gate checklist. This is planning support, not legal advice. Confirm all licensing, trust-accounting, contract, and employment questions with Washington counsel, the Washington Department of Licensing, and a qualified CPA before owner solicitation or property onboarding.

## Gate 1: Designated broker path

Status: blocked until confirmed.

Decision needed: who can legally serve as the firm's designated broker?

Required proof:

- Washington real estate managing broker license status for the proposed designated broker.
- Designated broker endorsement eligibility.
- Controlling interest structure for each firm where the person will be designated broker.
- Written answer on whether Megan can serve now, must obtain more credentials, must partner with a qualified DB, or must hire a qualified DB.

Current official references:

- Washington DOL managing broker license requirements: `https://dol.wa.gov/professional-licenses/real-estate-managing-brokers/get-your-license-real-estate-managing-broker`
- Washington DOL designated broker endorsement requirements: `https://dol.wa.gov/professional-licenses/real-estate-designated-brokers/get-your-endorsement-real-estate-designated-brokers`

Decision record to write:

| Question | Owner | Evidence needed | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| Does Megan hold an active Washington managing broker license? | Megan | DOL license lookup or license record | Pending |
| Does Megan qualify for designated broker endorsement? | Megan | Managing broker license + controlling interest plan | Pending |
| If not, is the fallback credential, partner, or hire? | Kevin/Megan | Cost, control, timeline, risk comparison | Pending |
| Can any PM sales/discovery occur before firm licensing? | Counsel | Written legal guidance | Pending |

## Gate 2: Firm and entity setup

Status: blocked until Gate 1 path is known.

Required proof:

- Firm name pre-approval path.
- Entity formation decision: single LLC at launch unless counsel recommends otherwise.
- UBI, business license, EIN, and real estate firm license sequence.
- Assumed-name/DBA decision if Green Lappe Properties differs from the legal entity name.
- Ownership/control structure aligned with designated broker requirements.

Current official reference:

- Washington DOL real estate firm license process: `https://dol.wa.gov/professional-licenses/real-estate-firms/get-your-license-real-estate-firms`

Output:

- One-page entity and licensing sequence.
- Go/no-go decision before any management agreement is signed.

## Gate 3: Trust accounting and client funds

Status: blocked until firm setup is in motion.

Required proof:

- Bank selection that can support Washington real estate trust accounting.
- Property-management trust account procedure.
- Security-deposit handling procedure.
- Monthly reconciliation owner and backup.
- PMS/trust-account integration decision.
- Written policy for rent, deposits, owner reserves, management fees, vendor payments, and bank charges.

Current official references:

- WAC 308-124E trust account procedures: `https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=308-124E`
- WAC 308-124E-105 general trust procedures: `https://app.leg.wa.gov/documents/LAWS/WACPDF/WAC%20308%20%20TITLE/WAC%20308%20-124E%20CHAPTER/WAC%20308%20-124E-105.pdf`

Output:

- Trust-account operations checklist.
- First-month reconciliation dry run before the first third-party rent collection.

Status: blocked until Kevin confirms obligations.

## Gate 7: Launch go/no-go

Proceed to first owner conversion only when all are true:

- Designated broker path is confirmed.
- Entity and firm licensing path is sequenced.
- Trust-account operating model is approved.
- Counsel has reviewed core contracts.
- Insurance path is quoted or bound.
- Owner discovery script is ready.
- First-cohort rejection criteria are explicit.

Do not proceed if:

- Designated broker path is unresolved.
- Trust-account handling is informal.
- Kevin's permitted role is unclear.
- The first owner must be accepted to keep momentum. A bad first property is worse than no first property.
