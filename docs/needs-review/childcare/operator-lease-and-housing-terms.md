# Operator Lease And Housing Terms

- Date: 2026-07-20
- Status: DRAFT
- Parent: [[../childcare-operator-property-model-strategy|Childcare Operator Property Model Strategy]]
- Implements: Operator relationship

## Objective

Define the agreement under which a licensed childcare operator lives in a GPS host home and runs the FCCH there, with subsidized housing as part of compensation tied to a written child-enrollment commitment, so incentives are aligned and effective vacancy is low.

## Scope

In scope: the operator agreement structure, the housing subsidy mechanism, the enrollment commitment, and the exit terms. Out of scope: the size of the revenue split (see revenue and enrollment share) and licensing (see licensing and compliance).

## Steps

1. Specify the housing component: how much of the operator's compensation is subsidized housing versus cash, and the enrollment floor that must be met for the full subsidy to apply.
2. Write the enrollment commitment as a measurable floor (filled child slots), with a defined measurement source that the operator cannot understate.
3. Define what happens when the floor is missed for a defined number of months: subsidy step-down, support plan, then exit.
4. Define exit and transition terms that protect enrolled families and the license continuity.

## Acceptance

- The agreement ties the housing subsidy to a measurable enrollment floor with a named measurement source.
- The agreement has a defined, humane path for a missed floor that ends in a clean exit if needed.

## Measures

- Operator retention at 12 and 24 months.
- Share of operating months in which the enrollment floor is met.

## Open questions

- D2 operator compensation shape and D3 revenue split must be set with the strategy owner before this agreement is finalized.
