# Target Market And ZIP Selection

- Date: 2026-07-20
- Status: DRAFT
- Parent: [[../childcare-operator-property-model-strategy|Childcare Operator Property Model Strategy]]
- Implements: Target market

## Objective

Select the ZIP clusters to enter first, using the market study's rule that the model works only for infant and toddler care in areas where families can pay and licensed infant supply is thin.

## Scope

In scope: a scored ZIP-selection method and the initial target list. Out of scope: individual property underwriting (see property sourcing and underwriting).

## Steps

1. For each candidate ZIP, gather median household income and an estimate of licensed infant slots per resident infant.
2. Apply the go rule from the market study: median household income above about $120,000 and fewer than one licensed infant slot per eight resident infants is a go.
3. Rank the go ZIPs and stage entry, starting with the highest-income, thinnest-supply clusters (Orange County: Irvine, South County, coastal), subject to D1.
4. Exclude working-class, subsidy-dependent ZIPs from the free-housing model; note them as a separate, later subsidy-anchored model.

## Acceptance

- A ranked target ZIP list exists, each entry carrying its income figure and its infant-slot-per-infant estimate and a go or no-go verdict.

## Measures

- Number of go ZIPs identified and entered.
- Per entered ZIP: realized operator fill rate versus the supply gap that justified entry.

## Open questions

- D1 geography decides whether these are Orange County ZIPs or Washington ZIPs; the method is identical, the data source is not.
