# Owner Discovery Guide

Purpose: prepare the 20 warm owner conversations needed before Green Lappe accepts a first cohort. These conversations test demand, pricing, trust transfer, and operating expectations. They are not sales calls unless legal launch gates allow sales activity.

## Target list

Start with Megan's warmest relationships:

| Segment | Why it matters | First filter |
|---|---|---|
| Recent buyers | May become future landlords or already hold another property. | Owns or may retain SFR/condo/townhome in Snohomish or north King. |
| Recent sellers | May have retained another home or know owners relocating. | Has held local property or is moving out of area. |
| Current owners relocating | Highest-intent accidental-landlord segment. | Wants to retain property but not self-manage. |
| Referral partners | Can surface owners before they search online. | Lenders, inspectors, attorneys, CPAs, contractors, agents. |
| Founding-property adjacent contacts | Fastest path to concrete operating feedback. | Knows Bothell/Snohomish corridor conditions. |

## Qualification screen

Capture this before a full discovery conversation:

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| Contact name | Relationship source and trust level. |
| Property location | ZIP, city, county, jurisdiction if known. |
| Property type | SFR, townhome, condo, small multifamily. |
| Owner situation | Relocating, accidental landlord, investor, inherited property, other. |
| Urgency | Now, 0-3 months, 3-12 months, someday. |
| Current management | Self-managed, incumbent PM, vacant, owner-occupied. |
| Pain trigger | Maintenance, tenant screening, compliance, rent collection, distance, time, trust. |
| Legal sensitivity | Seattle/local ordinance, HOA, deposit dispute, eviction, active lease issue. |
| Fit verdict | Strong fit, maybe, reject for now. |

## Conversation opener

Use relationship context first:

> "We're testing whether a small, owner-operated property management model would actually solve the problems local owners have with communication, fees, maintenance, and compliance. I'm not trying to sell you anything today. I want to understand what would make this useful or not useful for someone in your situation."

## Core questions

Ask these in plain language and record exact phrases when possible.

1. What would make you consider hiring a property manager?
2. What would make you avoid hiring one?
3. If you have used a PM before, what worked and what failed?
4. Which task would you most want off your plate: tenant placement, maintenance, compliance, rent collection, accounting, or resident communication?
5. What would you expect to see in a monthly owner update?
6. How much control do you want over maintenance approvals?
7. How do you feel about maintenance markups if invoices are transparent?
8. Would you prefer a flat monthly fee or a percent of rent? Why?
9. What is the maximum fee that would still feel fair if service quality was high?
10. What proof would you need before trusting a new firm with your property?
11. Would Megan's direct involvement change your trust level?
12. What would make you refer another owner?
13. What would cause you to fire a PM in the first 90 days?
14. Do you have a property or owner situation that could become a first pilot case?

## Pricing test

Do not pitch a final price. Test reactions to postures:

| Option | How to frame it | What to listen for |
|---|---|---|
| Flat-fee premium | "One fixed monthly management fee, clear placement fee, no hidden maintenance markup." | Trust, simplicity, concern about high-maintenance homes. |
| Premium percentage | "A familiar percentage of rent, but with transparent invoices and no surprise maintenance economics." | Comparability, commodity perception, willingness to pay. |
| Hybrid guardrail | "Base fee plus explicit approval thresholds and pass-through costs." | Whether owners value predictability or flexibility more. |

## Evidence to collect

Each conversation should produce:

- Fit score from 1 to 5.
- Timing score from 1 to 5.
- Pricing reaction.
- Top pain.
- Trust proof needed.
- Referral likelihood.
- Quote that can inform positioning.
- Next step or reason to disqualify.

## First-cohort rejection criteria

Reject or defer owners when any of these are true:

- Owner wants Green Lappe to solve an active legal dispute before launch readiness is complete.
- Owner expects undisclosed fee games or below-market pricing.
- Property has unresolved safety, habitability, title, HOA, or insurance issues.
- Owner will not respect written maintenance approval thresholds.
- Property is outside the launch geography without a strategic reason.
- The only reason to accept is momentum.

## Success threshold

The discovery motion is strong enough to proceed when:

- 20 conversations are complete.
- At least 5 owners are high-fit/high-timing.
- At least 2-5 properties are credible first-cohort candidates.
- Pricing feedback supports one launch pricing posture.
- The top trust proof is clear enough to shape the owner packet.
- The first systems artifact can be chosen from observed owner pain, not internal preference.
