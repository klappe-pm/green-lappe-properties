# Launch Prioritization

## TL;DR

Launch is Snohomish County. Single-family homes are the target. Megan Green's existing relationships with buyers, sellers, and current owners who want out of Snohomish County (but want to keep the property) are the first-cohort source. King County is phase two; the county line is a soft barrier, not a hard one, because many owners hold properties in both counties and can be referred across. FCC is the margin-expansion wedge, not the launch product. The brand promises something different than incumbents, and the launch has to prove that promise with two-to-five real properties inside year one.

## 1. Priority

|Phase|Geography|Inventory target|Timeline|
|---|---|---|---|
|`phase-1-launch`|Snohomish County only|2–5 single-family homes under management|Months `1`–`12`|
|`phase-2-expand`|Add King County|6–12 single-family homes under management|Months `13`–`24`|
|`phase-3-wedge`|Both counties, FCC overlay|1–3 FCC-converted properties|Months `18`–`30`|

Phases overlap. The FCC wedge can be initiated in phase 1 if an owner relationship presents the right property, but the volume product (standard residential) must be operating at acceptable quality before the wedge takes management bandwidth.

## 2. Why Snohomish County, and why Single-family

### 2.1 The Megan Green Network

The launch is not a cold-start. Megan Green has documented existing relationships in Snohomish County across three counterparty types:

|Counterparty type|What they want|How Green Lappe serves them|
|---|---|---|
|`recent-buyer`|Bought a home, may turn it into a rental at some point.|First-call relationship when conversion to rental happens.|
|`recent-seller`|Sold a home, often relocating. May have other properties they did not sell.|First-call relationship for those retained properties.|
|`current-owner-relocating`|Owns a home in Snohomish County, moving out of the area (geographically or professionally), wants to retain the property as an investment but cannot self-manage from a distance.|Direct primary customer for the launch product.|

The third group is the launch ICP. It is the highest-intent segment available without paid acquisition.

### 2.2 Why Single-family Homes Specifically

- Megan's transaction history is concentrated in single-family homes; the network is the network.
- Single-family is the same asset class as the founding Bothell property, so operational templates transfer cleanly.
- Single-family rentals are the most common ownership pattern among accidental-landlord and small-portfolio-investor segments (the [[green-lappe-two-sided-marketplace|marketplace supply segments]]).
- Single-family is the property type that maps to the FCC wedge later. Conversion path is preserved.
- Multifamily, condos with restrictive HOAs, and commercial property are explicitly out of scope for phase 1.

### 2.3 Why Snohomish before King

- Megan's network density in Snohomish exceeds King.
- The Bothell property is already in Snohomish (98012 unincorporated). Operational competence on the founding property accrues to Snohomish first.
- Snohomish County PDS, fire marshal, and DCYF regional staff become known counterparties through the first conversions.
- Lower competitive density of property managers actively differentiated on transparency and SLAs in Snohomish than in central King County.

### 2.4 Why the County Line is a Soft Barrier

- Owners frequently hold property in both counties. An owner whose Snohomish home is managed well will refer or transfer their Lynnwood, Bellevue, or Kirkland property.
- The drive radius from Bothell covers a meaningful slice of north King County (Kenmore, Lake Forest Park, Shoreline) without changing the operational model.
- Phase-2 expansion is a marketing decision, not an infrastructure decision. The same staff, vendors, software, and templates work across both counties.
- The reason to draw a line at all is focus during phase 1. Marketing in two counties dilutes the message. Operating in two counties from day one stretches bandwidth.

## 3. Launch Leverage Points (where the Megan Green Relationship Matters most)

|Leverage point|Why it matters at launch|What it makes possible|
|---|---|---|
|Warm introductions to owners|Cold acquisition cost for property management is high and slow. Warm intros bypass it.|First 5 owners signed without paid marketing.|
|Transactional context|Megan already knows the property, the owner's situation, and the financial picture.|Faster qualification, fewer mismatches, lower onboarding cost.|
|Trust transfer|Owners trust Megan as their real estate professional. The brand inherits that trust on day one.|Higher conversion from intro to signed management agreement.|
|Referral graph|One satisfied owner with multiple properties or peer owners produces compounding referrals.|Marketing budget approaches zero during phase 1.|
|Local market knowledge|Megan knows rent comps, condition baselines, neighborhood dynamics.|Better pricing, better screening, better retention.|

## 4. What Launch Success Looks like

Concrete criteria, all measurable, evaluated at month `12`.

|Metric|Phase-1 target|Verdict|
|---|---|---|
|Properties under management|2–5|Below 2 means the hypothesis is broken. Above 5 means scaling pressure earlier than expected.|
|Owner retention at 12-month mark|`100%` of phase-1 cohort|The promise is small portfolio, named contact, personal service. Any owner loss in year one means the promise broke.|
|Resident retention at lease renewal|`≥ 70%`|Renewal rate is the strongest signal of resident-side delight.|
|Habitability SLA adherence|`≥ 95%` of tickets resolved or explained inside SLA|The brand pillar of `responsive` is enforceable here.|
|Owner-facing fee disputes|`0`|Transparent pricing means no surprise fees. Any dispute is a process failure.|
|Net Promoter–style signal from owners|Unprompted referral count `≥ 2`|Word of mouth is the only acceptable channel for phase 1.|
|Net Promoter–style signal from residents|Renewal at full market rent without push-back|Tested at first renewal cycle.|
|Time to onboard a new property|`≤ 30 days` from signed agreement to first rent payment|Operational speed test.|

## 5. What Gets Explicitly Deferred

The following are real opportunities but are out of scope for phase 1. Deferring them is a feature, not a defect.

- King County properties not adjacent to the Bothell base.
- Multifamily and small commercial.
- HOA management.
- Property acquisitions by the company itself (PropCo owning inventory).
- A full FCC operator-as-a-service play (Structure C from [[green-lappe-fcc-strategy|FCC Strategy]]).
- Public-facing listings portal.
- Paid marketing channels.
- Out-of-state owner pursuit beyond Megan's existing network.
- Custom software development beyond off-the-shelf PM platform configuration.

## 7. Risk-adjusted Launch Decisions

|Decision|Recommendation|Reasoning|
|---|---|---|
|Single LLC vs PropCo / OpCo split|Single LLC at launch.|Lower complexity. Switch to split when scale or FCC activity demands it.|
|Flat fee vs percent of rent|Flat fee per property per month.|Transparency. Pricing signals brand promise. Reconsider at 10+ doors.|
|Megan or Kevin signs management agreements|Megan signs.|Brand is built around her. Trust transfer is to her.|
|Whether to take the founding property under the new entity immediately|Yes, on the next clean transition.|Brand should manage its own founding property. Eat the dog food.|
|Whether to publish listings to public|No at phase 1.|Listings stay invitation-based until cohort quality is proven.|
|Whether to accept the first FCC opportunity if offered in phase 1|Yes, if a qualified provider and a qualified property arrive together.|The wedge is the long-term differentiator. Do not turn down validated demand.|
|Whether to discount fees for phase-1 cohort|No.|Discounting trains the market to expect discounts. Brand promise justifies the price.|

## 8. What This Memo Does Not Decide

These remain open and need separate decisions:

1. Domain name (`greenlappe.com` to be verified for availability).
2. PM software vendor selection (Buildium vs AppFolio vs Rentvine).
3. Counsel selection for WA landlord-tenant work.
4. Insurance broker selection.
5. Whether the founding property uses the existing Bothell tenant relationship as the test case for new SLA standards, or waits for the next turnover to apply them.
6. Whether to begin a referral incentive program in phase 1 or wait until phase 2.
