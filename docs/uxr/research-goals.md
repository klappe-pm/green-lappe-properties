# Research Goals

Scope: understand and reuse the existing Green Property Management corpus. No web search. Treat the repo as the source of truth unless a future task explicitly authorizes external verification.

## Primary Goal

Convert the existing research, design, GTM, metrics, and funding notes into partner-ready materials for Megan: a two-pager, a pitch deck, and a market deep dive that make the opportunity, assumptions, and decision path clear.

## Audience Goal

Megan does not need a cold introduction to the pain points or opportunity. She needs a crisp read on whether the project is investable in time, reputation, and partnership focus. The materials should make it easier for her to say:

1. This is the right wedge.
2. This is the right first market.
3. This is the right role split.
4. These are the assumptions we must prove before scaling.
5. These are the decisions we need to make next.

## Research Questions to Answer from the Corpus

1. What is the largest defensible market number, and what smaller number is more useful for a Megan partnership decision?
2. Which launch plan is commercially rational even if it is not the highest ranked ZCTA plan?
3. Which assumptions are facts, which are modeled, and which are still decisions?
4. How does the company avoid sounding like a commodity PM firm?
5. What proof would make the two-principal model credible inside 60 to 90 days?
6. What must be omitted or caveated because the corpus does not prove it yet?

## Output Rules

- Keep claims linked to source files.
- Label modeled figures as modeled.
- Reconcile conflicting scale lenses rather than hiding them.
- Use exact numbers where the corpus provides them.
- Use bounded ranges where the corpus itself uses ranges.
- Do not claim current external facts without a future web verification pass.
