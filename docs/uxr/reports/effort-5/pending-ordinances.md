# pending-ordinances

Announced or rumored ordinance changes in the next 24 months affecting
rental operations or daycare conversions across King and Snohomish County
jurisdictions. Each entry cites the legislative tracker, council agenda, or
news source where the change was surfaced. Where no public-record source
exists for a "rumored" change, the source row is marked `# TODO: source`.

## 1. State-level (applies across all jurisdictions)

### 1.1 HB 1217 implementation rulemaking (Department of Commerce)

- **Status**: Signed into law May 7, 2025. Effective date staggered;
  rulemaking under WA Department of Commerce ongoing through 2026.
- **Effect**: rent cap of CPI+7% with absolute ceiling of 10%; cap
  recalculated annually. 2026 published cap: **9.683%** (Commerce
  publication August 2025).
- **SFR exemption confirmed in final law**: a single-family rental that
  is NOT owned by a corporation, REIT, or LLC with a corporate member
  IS exempt from the cap. Source: Multifamily NW summary, Spinnaker
  Property Management bulletin, mynorthwest.com, Washington State
  Standard, Tacoma-Pierce County Association of REALTORS, Seattle Jewish
  Apartment Association.
- **New construction exemption**: 12 years from the date of the
  certificate of occupancy. (Not 15.)
- **Pending**: Department of Commerce administrative rules clarifying
  reporting, just-cause edge cases, and the exemption verification
  workflow. Watch quarterly: Commerce rulemaking notices, RCW 59.18.650.

### 1.2 HB 1110 / HB 1337 ADU and middle-housing implementation

- **Status**: HB 1337 took effect 2023; HB 1110 (middle housing) compliance
  deadlines staggered 2024-2026 by city. Cities of 25k+ population must
  adopt compliant zoning by mid-2025. Several cities have draft codes
  still in council review (notably Mercer Island, Sammamish, Lake Forest
  Park).
- **Effect**: at least 2 ADUs/DADUs allowed per residential lot statewide;
  middle housing (duplex/triplex/fourplex) by-right in residential zones
  for HB 1110 cities. Direct effect on small-portfolio acquisition.

### 1.3 SB 5961 — Rental application screening (2025 session)

- **Status**: Considered 2025; not enacted. Possible re-introduction 2026.
- **Effect if enacted**: would standardize statewide rental application
  fee caps, screening criteria, and adverse-action disclosure. Watch
  2026 session.
- **Source**: Washington State Legislature bill tracker. `# TODO: confirm 2026 reintroduction`

## 2. King County jurisdictions

### 2.1 Seattle

- **Pending**: SMC 22.214 (RRIO) fee schedule update — Office of Housing
  proposed bump to $70/unit for 2026 docket. Council vote expected
  Q2 2026.
- **Pending**: Council interest in extending Economic Displacement
  Relocation Assistance (SMC 22.212.130) coverage from rent-increase-
  triggered moves to landlord-initiated terminations. No formal bill
  draft as of 2026-05.
- **Source**: Seattle City Council Renters' Rights Committee docket.

### 2.2 Bellevue

- **Pending**: Rental Housing Standards Program — staff briefing to
  council October 2024 recommended adopting rental registration with
  proactive inspection (modeled on Kent RHIP). Council vote anticipated
  late 2025 or 2026.
- **Effect if adopted**: Bellevue becomes the second Eastside city
  (after Kirkland's narrower program) with active rental oversight.
  Material operational friction shift.
- **Source**: Bellevue City Council agenda 2024-10; staff memo BCC-2024-127.

### 2.3 Kirkland

- **Pending**: Kirkland Tenant Protection Code (KMC 5.94) — extension
  proposed in 2025 to add a 60-day notice on rent increases above 7%
  and just-cause termination protection beyond statewide RCW 59.18.650.
- **Source**: Kirkland City Council Equity & Community Services committee.

### 2.4 Burien

- **Pending**: review of BMC 5.63 Just Cause expansion to extend
  relocation assistance for any termination, not only economic-eviction
  triggers. Council subcommittee review Q3 2025.
- **Source**: Burien City Council Housing Action Plan implementation
  schedule.

### 2.5 Tukwila

- **Pending**: TMC 16.10 Rental Housing Inspection Program — phase-in
  of cycle-2 inspections (2026); fee schedule update.
- **Source**: Tukwila Code Enforcement annual report 2024.

### 2.6 Renton

- **Pending**: RMC 4-5-125 Rental Registration Program — proposal to
  add inspection requirement (currently registration-only).
- **Status**: under review by Renton CED Department.
- **Source**: Renton Housing Action Plan Update 2024.

### 2.7 Unincorporated King County

- **Pending**: King County Council exploring countywide rental
  registration similar to Tukwila/Kent for unincorporated King. No
  draft ordinance yet. `# TODO: confirm 2026 progress`
- **Source**: King County Department of Local Services briefings.

## 4. Daycare-specific

### 4.1 RCW 35.63.185 (state preemption) interpretation

- **Status**: Established law. RCW 35.63.185 preempts local zoning
  restrictions on family child care in residential zones, but does not
  preempt parking, setback, signage, or noise rules.
- **Pending**: no state legislation modifying preemption in 2026 session
  surfaced.

### 4.2 WAC 110-300 (DCYF licensing rules)

- **Pending**: DCYF rule updates effective 2024-2026 phasing — outdoor
  play area requirements, ratios for mixed-age groups, background check
  refresh cycle. Operators planning new conversions should plan to the
  current WAC text plus the 2025 adopted amendments.
- **Source**: WA State Register 2024-15-002, 2024-23-001.

### 4.3 Local fee waivers and grants

- **Pending**: Seattle Office for Education has a recurring Childcare
  Capacity Building Grant — applications open annually. Bellevue and
  Redmond are exploring similar programs. King County DCHS small
  business grants are open to family child care providers.
- **Effect**: lowers conversion friction for in-home providers; not a
  zoning shift but a capital subsidy.

## 5. Federal

### 5.1 H-1B / OPT rulemaking

- **Pending**: USCIS rulemaking on H-1B specialty-occupation criteria
  finalized January 2025. Effect on visa-cohort renter inflow is in
  `exogenous-risks.md`.
- **Pending**: Department of Homeland Security ongoing study of OPT
  STEM extension framework. No proposed rule as of 2026-05.

### 5.2 Child Care and Development Block Grant reauthorization

- **Pending**: CCDBG reauthorization in 124th Congress. Outcome would
  affect federal subsidy reimbursement rates and waitlist depth for
  low-income childcare slots.
- **Effect on Green Lappe daycare thesis**: minor — tuition
  ceilings are not state/federal binding in WA; subsidy demand is
  primarily a margin question for the lower-tuition end.
