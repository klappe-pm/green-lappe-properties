# 01-positioning

Strategic positioning for Green Property Management. The foundation doc that every other design system decision references. Consumed by brand, design, content, and engineering executors when evaluating whether a downstream decision aligns with the brand.

## Dependencies

None. This is the source doc.

## Outputs

1. The one-paragraph definition of what Green PM is
2. The two-audience conversion model
3. The anti-positioning table
4. The competitive whitespace map
5. The four ranked differentiators

## 1.1 What Green PM is

A property management company for small landlords (1-to-20-door portfolios) in King and Snohomish counties, Washington. Megan Green, designated broker, is the named operator. 9% of collected rent. 60% of one month for leasing fee, billed on placement only. No setup fees, no maintenance markup.

## 1.2 What the brand has to do

Convert two skeptical audiences into customers, then serve them reliably:

1. Small landlords burned by national chains, REIT-style PM, or unreliable solo operators
2. Renters scanning fifty Zillow listings looking for something that doesn't feel like Greystar or Craigslist

Once converted, both audiences need operational surfaces that feel reliable and frictionless, not seductive.

## 1.3 What the brand is not (anti-positioning)

Not corporate. Not luxury. Not a tech startup. Not a national franchise. Not pretending to be bigger than it is. Not signaling institutional-asset-operator gravitas.

## 1.4 Competitive whitespace

| Archetype | Visual signature | Weakness |
|---|---|---|
| Legacy PM (Bell-Anderson, Cornell, Brink) | Navy, serif, corporate | Cold, outdated, interchangeable |
| Apartment REITs | Grayscale, sterile | Institutional, generic |
| Boutique lifestyle PM | Sage, beige, sans-serif | Unserious operationally |
| SaaS proptech | Gradient, geometric, "AI-powered" | Impersonal, scale-signaling |
| Green PM | Cedar plus Cream/Paper plus Clay, named operator, grotesque type system | High-trust regional operator with modern systems |

## 1.5 Ranked differentiators

Every brand decision must reinforce at least one of these:

1. **Named accountability.** One owner, one phone number, one face. Megan.
2. **Compliance fluency.** HB 1217, Seattle Just Cause, 180-day notice, Issaquah, Tacoma, Burien stack handled.
3. **Small-portfolio bias.** Built for 1-to-20-door landlords, not institutional clients.
4. **Plain-language reporting.** No PDFs nobody reads. No quarterly mystery statements.

## 1.6 Geographic scope

- **Primary**: King County and Snohomish County, Washington
- **Cities served**: Seattle, Bothell, Kirkland, Edmonds, Shoreline, Lynnwood, Redmond, Mountlake Terrace, and contiguous communities within roughly a 90-minute drive of a managed property
- **Out of scope**: Properties outside King or Snohomish counties; commercial buildings; institutional asset operators

## 1.7 Pricing position

Fixed, transparent, declared on every owner-facing surface:

- 9% of collected rent (management fee)
- 60% of one month's rent (leasing fee, billed only on placement)
- $0 setup fee
- $0 maintenance markup

Pricing is part of positioning. Hiding it is on-brand for legacy PM and off-brand for Green PM.

## Acceptance

This doc is acceptable when:

- A downstream agent can answer "is X on-brand for Green PM?" by referencing one or more sections here
- The four ranked differentiators are unambiguous
- The anti-positioning is clear enough to reject a font, color, photo, or copy line that drifts toward any of the four refused archetypes
