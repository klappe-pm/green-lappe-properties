# 75-social-media

The social media spec. Which platforms to maintain, how to set up each profile, what content to post, what visuals to use, and what to never post. Consumed by Megan (content author), the content scheduling tool, and any future contractor producing social content.

## Dependencies

- `01-positioning` for the named-operator, regional, anti-corporate position
- `03-voice` for the calm, specific, direct tone
- `04-microcopy` for approved phrases and forbidden banned-patterns
- `61-photography` for what photographs are appropriate
- `62-illustration` for illustration when it appears

## Outputs

1. Platform strategy (which platforms, why)
2. Profile setup specifications per platform
3. Content patterns (what to post)
4. Visual template specifications per platform
5. Posting cadence
6. Engagement and response rules
7. Crisis communication rules
8. Forbidden social patterns

## Strategic frame

Social media for Green PM is a low-priority channel. The named-operator positioning depends on Megan being personally available, not on building a social audience. The strategy is:

1. **Maintain credible-looking profiles** on platforms where small landlords and renters search
2. **Post infrequently with substance**, not frequently with noise
3. **Never use social as the primary marketing channel**
4. **Never let social drive operational decisions**

Anti-strategy: do not chase virality, do not jump on trends, do not use Reels with trending audio, do not partner with influencers, do not run paid social campaigns in the brand's first year.

## Content patterns

Eight content patterns the brand uses on social. Mix to taste; do not repeat the same pattern back-to-back.

### Pattern 1: New listing announcement

> A new rental on Aurora Ave N just listed.
> 2 bed, 1 bath. $2,200 a month. Available March 1.
> Drive-by friendly; let me know if you want a private showing.
> [Listing link]

Photo: exterior wide shot. Single photo, no carousel.

### Pattern 2: Field note

A short version of a blog post field note. One specific story, one specific outcome.

> The boiler at a 1924 four-plex on 18th Ave NE is 22 years old. Replacement today: $3,500. Replacement during a January cold snap when it dies: $5,500 plus emergency dispatch.
> Owner approved the replacement last week. Work starts Tuesday.

Photo: contextual (the boiler, the basement, the property exterior).

### Pattern 3: Compliance reminder

A neighborly heads-up about an upcoming compliance window.

> HB 1217 caps rent increases statewide at 7% plus CPI, with a hard cap of 10%. For most King County properties this year, the effective cap is 5.1%.
> If you're considering a renewal raise, run the numbers against the cap before you draft the notice.

No photo; or a hand-drawn rate-cap diagram from `62-illustration.md`.

### Pattern 4: Process glimpse

A short look at what Megan does between collecting rent.

> Spent this morning walking a vacant rental in Edmonds with the owner. New flooring, paint, and a re-keyed front door before listing. Two weeks of vacancy, then back online at $2,650.

Photo: process photo (Megan in the unit, or a detail shot of the work).

### Pattern 5: Vendor recognition

Specific praise for a vendor who did good work.

> North Seattle Gutters got two of my properties cleared this week ahead of the November rains. Fair pricing, quick scheduling, and they sent photos when done.

No mandatory photo. Tag the vendor's business profile when the platform supports it.

### Pattern 6: Q&A

Answer a real question Megan gets from owners or renters.

> "Can my landlord raise rent in the middle of my lease?"
> No. The lease term is the rent term. Raises happen at renewal, with the proper notice period (90 days in most King and Snohomish jurisdictions).

No photo or a hand-drawn diagram.

### Pattern 7: Local recognition

Acknowledge a neighborhood, business, or event that's part of the brand's regional identity.

> Edmonds Arts Festival is this weekend. If you're driving down to see it and want to peek at a rental nearby, two are showing on Saturday.

Use sparingly. The brand is a property manager, not a tourism account.

### Pattern 8: Year-in-review / quarter-in-review

Once or twice a year, a substantive look at portfolio performance and lessons.

> 2026 in review: 14 properties under management, 2 new owners onboarded, 11 lease renewals, 3 turnovers. Average vacancy: 14 days. Three major capital projects (a roof, a boiler, a sewer line). Read the full post on the blog.

Link to a long-form blog post.

## Posting cadence

| Platform | Target | Maximum |
|---|---|---|
| Google Business Profile | 1 post per week | 2 per week |
| LinkedIn | 2 posts per month | 1 per week |
| Instagram | 2 posts per month | 1 per week |
| Nextdoor | 1 post per month | 2 per month |
| Yelp | Profile updates only; no scheduled posts | n/a |

Quality over frequency. A month with two excellent posts beats a month with twelve filler posts.

## Engagement and response rules

### Comments and DMs

- Respond within one business day during business hours
- Respond as Megan (first person)
- Keep responses short and specific
- Move complex conversations off-platform (email or phone)

### Reviews

| Review tone | Response approach |
|---|---|
| 5-star detailed | Reply with thanks plus a specific detail acknowledging the relationship; do not gush |
| 5-star short | Reply with thanks; one sentence |
| 4-star | Reply with thanks; address any constructive criticism specifically |
| 3-star | Reply with thanks for feedback; address the issue concretely; offer to discuss off-platform |
| 1-2 star | Reply with calm acknowledgment; do not argue; offer to discuss off-platform; do not contradict facts in public |

Sample 5-star response:

> Thanks, John. Glad the boiler replacement worked out smoothly, Anderson Plumbing was the right call. Talk soon.

Sample 1-star response:

> Thanks for taking the time to write. I'd like to understand more about what didn't work. Could you email me at megan@greenpmpnw.com? Happy to talk through it.

### What never gets responded to

- Spam comments
- Comments from automated accounts
- Comments that demand the brand's position on unrelated political subjects
- Comments that attempt to bait into public arguments

### Reposting and sharing

- Share content from local government accounts (city of Seattle, King County, WA RentMan resources) when it's directly useful to owners or renters
- Share content from vendors only with their permission and only if the content is genuinely useful
- Never share content from competitors
- Never share generic motivational content
- Never share political content

## Crisis communication

If something goes wrong (a property fire, a tenant safety issue, a major service outage), social is not the primary channel. The primary channel is direct outreach to affected parties.

Social posts during a crisis:

- One brief acknowledgment that the brand is aware and acting
- Direction to the primary information channel (email, phone, the website)
- No speculation
- No blame
- No commentary on individuals

After the crisis is resolved:

- One factual update
- No claim of having handled it perfectly
- Acknowledge what was learned, if relevant

## Forbidden social patterns

- Hashtag stuffing (more than three relevant hashtags per post is forbidden)
- Engagement-bait phrases ("Tag a landlord who needs to see this!", "Double-tap if you agree")
- Reels with trending audio
- Dancing or lip-sync videos
- Memes
- Stock photo posts ("Happy National Property Management Day!")
- Manufactured holidays
- "Sliding into DMs" voice in business interactions
- Capitalizing for emphasis in body copy
- Auto-DM on follow
- Asking for follows in posts
- Generic "we appreciate you" testimonial repost graphics
- AI-generated images
- AI-generated text without editorial review by Megan
- Influencer partnerships
- Sponsored content
- Vague motivational quotes about real estate or success
- Politically-loaded content
- Sales-funnel-style posts ("Want my five secrets to..." / "DM me 'YES' to receive...")

## Acceptance

This doc is acceptable when:

- Every platform listed has a documented profile setup
- Every content post Megan publishes can be mapped to one of the eight patterns
- Every visual asset uses an approved template
- Response rules for reviews, comments, and DMs are clear
- Forbidden patterns are concrete enough that an outside contractor cannot accidentally produce off-brand content
