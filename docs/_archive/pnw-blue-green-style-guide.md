# PNW Blue Green Style Guide

Purpose: enforce a consistent Pacific Northwest blue/green system across the partner deck and related opportunity materials.

## Design Principles

1. Quiet confidence over startup gloss.
2. Overcast Pacific Northwest light, not tropical teal.
3. Data-led, with clear proof objects on every slide.
4. Minimal decoration; use lines, bands, tables, and maps as structure.
5. Warm enough for a partner conversation, rigorous enough for an investor memo.

## Palette

| Token | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| `evergreen` | `#173A2B` | Primary text on light surfaces; dark backgrounds. |
| `salal` | `#2F5D50` | Primary accent, bars, section labels. |
| `sound-blue` | `#226A7A` | Secondary accent, comparison bars, market scale. |
| `rain-blue` | `#5F8591` | Muted supporting marks and map water tone. |
| `moss` | `#8FAE82` | Positive or future-state highlight. |
| `cedar` | `#9A6A3A` | Financial or owner-economics accent. |
| `fog` | `#E9EEE9` | Slide background. |
| `paper` | `#FAFAF6` | Light surface and table fill. |
| `basalt` | `#26302B` | Dark neutral, footers, labels. |
| `warning` | `#B74E3B` | Risk flags only. |

## Typography

Use system-safe fonts unless the deck is later rebuilt in a brand environment.

| Role | Typeface | Size guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Slide title | Georgia or Fraunces if installed | 32-46 pt |
| Body | Avenir Next or Inter if installed | 13-18 pt |
| Tables and labels | Avenir Next or Inter | 9-12 pt |
| Data callouts | Georgia bold or Avenir Next demi | 22-42 pt |

## Slide Grammar

Each slide must contain:

1. A claim in the title.
2. One proof object: chart, table, funnel, map, timeline, scorecard, or decision matrix.
3. A quiet footer pointer to source or assumption class.

Avoid:

- Decorative card grids.
- Stock photos.
- House/key/leaf icons.
- Full-slide prose.
- A slide that only repeats known pain points without creating a decision.

## Layout Families

Use at least five macro-layouts in a 12-slide deck:

| Layout | Best for |
|---|---|
| Cover with metric rail | Opening the business case. |
| Two-column tension | DataStory current/future contrast. |
| Horizontal bar proof | TAM/SAM/SOM and financial scale. |
| Funnel | First 50 doors and relationship conversion. |
| Operating system map | Systems leverage and workflow. |
| Timeline | 30/60/90-day plan and launch sequencing. |
| Risk register | Assumptions and open questions. |
| Appendix table | Source and caveat traceability. |

## Data Styling

- Use `sound-blue` for total market.
- Use `salal` for serviceable or focused opportunity.
- Use `cedar` for revenue, margin, and cost.
- Use `moss` for future-state or validated proof.
- Use `warning` only for open risks.
- Label bars directly. Do not rely on legends when labels can sit on marks.

## Voice

Use plain language. Megan is a partner, not an audience to be sold with hype.

Good:

- "The market is large, but the launch should be narrow."
- "Snohomish is the proof ground; King is the expansion layer."
- "FCC is the margin wedge, not the first product."

Avoid:

- "Disrupt property management."
- "AI-powered real estate platform."
- "Capture massive TAM."
