# Why This Recommendation

The surname-as-PM-brand path is the default move, and it is wrong for three reasons that compound over a 5 to 10 year horizon.

**First, "Green" is doing two jobs that fight each other.** As a surname it is a trust signal; as a modifier it is a sustainability claim. Every piece of marketing has to disambiguate which job it is doing. The brand pays a tax on every impression. Worse, the sustainability reading is the default for any reader who does not already know Megan, which is every reader at the top of the funnel. A surname only works as a brand prefix when the surname is uncommon enough to be unambiguous (Sotheby's, Coldwell, Windermere) or when the person is already locally famous (Compass agent teams). Green is neither.

**Second, the PM business and the real estate business have opposite brand physics.** Property management is a recurring, operational, low-glamour service where trust is built through reliability, communication, and not screwing up. The brand should feel competent, calm, and infrastructural. Real estate sales is a transactional, high-stakes, personality-driven business where trust is built through the agent. The brand is the person. These two brand systems do not share visual language, voice, or positioning. Trying to make one surname carry both means one of them gets compromised. Almost always the PM brand wins the visual fight (because it has more surface area, more touchpoints, more content) and the sales brand inherits a voice tuned for a different job.

**Third, optionality is asymmetric.** If the PM business uses a neutral brand and Megan later launches Green Realty, the PM brand is unaffected and the real estate brand starts clean. If the PM business uses "Green" and Megan later launches Green Realty, the real estate brand inherits whatever reputation the PM business has built, good or bad, and every renter who had a bad maintenance experience is now a poisoned lead for the sales business. The downside is bounded on one path and unbounded on the other. Pick the bounded path.

The recommendation, in one sentence: build the PM business under a non-surname brand engineered for operational trust, and hold "Green" entirely in reserve for Megan's future real estate work.
